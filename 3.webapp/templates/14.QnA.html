Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and behave.

Good mental health allows individuals to manage stress, work productively, and connect meaningfully with others.

Common signs include: Feeling sad or down for long periods, Extreme mood changes or irritability, Withdrawal from social activities, Changes in eating or sleeping habits, Difficulty concentrating or making decisions, Excessive fears, worries, or guilt.

To manage stress: Practice mindfulness or meditation, Exercise regularly, Maintain a healthy work-life balance, Talk to friends or family about your feelings, Avoid overloading yourself with tasks.

Chronic anxiety may require professional help. You can: Practice deep breathing exercises, Avoid caffeine and alcohol, Try grounding techniques, like focusing on your five senses, Speak to a mental health professional for guidance.
